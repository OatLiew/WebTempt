Technology

This Small Business Innovation Research Phase I project aims to develop the technology necessary in order to realize a novel, energy saving device for filtering air in large commercial buildings. The goal is to develop a highly efficient dust collection and immobilization scheme that exploits the electrical and microscale structural properties of novel foam materials in order to create a cost and energy saving alternative to the common fiber mesh filter.

In commercial building ventilation, approximately 80% of the costs are directed at air filtration – both the cost of the consumables, as well as the energy required to transport outdoor air through the filtration, cooling and heating equipment, to the occupied indoor space. The majority of the energy is used in overcoming the backpressure generated by the mesh fiber filters that remove dust particles from the incoming air. Efforts to replace fiber mesh filters with ones that provide a lower backpressure, such as traditional electrostatic and polarization air cleaners, have been unsuccessful due to them not being able to meet the basic filtration performance requirements in an economical fashion. This project aims to resolve these deficiencies.

The Objectives

-Optimize polymer foam dust collecting material for maximum filtration efficiency.

-Optimize polymer foam dust collecting material for dust holding capacity.

-Minimize airflow resistance caused by foam dust collecting material.
