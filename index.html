Innovative Approach

Critical technology that enables a novel, energy-saving electronic air filtration mechanism

Cost Saving

In commercial building ventilation, approximately 80% of the costs are directed at air filtration.

Energy Efficient

Significantly reduce the energy usage of large commercial buildings, while providing a substantial financial incentive for wide adoption



Technology

Our technology addresses the downsides of such existing filtration products, namely prohibitively high costs of installation and maintenance, and enables the creation of a device that can be installed directly in place of existing fiber mesh filters, requiring minimal installation and upkeep expenditures. Being able to access the retrofit market allows this technology to make a greater global environmental impact by significantly reducing the energy usage of all types of commercial buildings, regardless of age or existing energy conserving features.

Our Team

The project team met in 2012 through the University of Washington’s Center for Commercialization (C4C). All current team members are working for “sweat equity”; there are no employees. The team does not yet have a commercial product so there is no revenue history. Dr. Igor Krichtafovitch formerly served as an executive with Kronos Air Technologies, which previously brought an ionic filtration device to the consumer market.
